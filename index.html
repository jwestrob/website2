Jacob West-Roberts

Computational biologist building AI systems for weird biology.

I work on microbes, massive datasets, and the kind of proteins that make structure predictors nervous. A lot of my time is spent wiring up AI agents, models, and tooling for bioinformatic analysis. I work to make messy genomic data explorable and fold difficult proteins.

I have a strong drive to find ways to leverage novel biology to develop environmental bioremediation techniques, especially in the pursuit of cleaning up mine pollution in the mountains of Colorado.

Most of my work goes into designing AI-driven analysis workflows. In my spare time, I love making generative mathematical art (see the Art page!) and hunting for cool rocks.
